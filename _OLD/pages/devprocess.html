Development Process

Overview

Our development process is broken down into three distinct parts:

  1. Design,
  2. Development, and
  3. Testing

Each of these parts is broken down further into 1 week chunks called 'sprints' in line with our agile methodology.

Design

As its name suggests, the Design stage is when all the design documents are drafted. This includes technical documents of the software's internal workings, and drafts of the user interface. Once created, we'll get your approval on the wireframes.

Development

The development stage usually takes the longest. During this time, we work hard to build your software to the agreed specifications. As we develop, we test each individual feature to ensure it functions as required.

Throughout this stage, we'll provide regular updates on our progress. From time to time, we're happy to arrange meetings to provide a live demonstration of our progress to ensure it is exactly what you envisioned.

Testing

Although we carry out testing throughout the development process, we carry out much more extensive testing once the final product is complete to ensure features don't cause issues when working together. Once the software passes all our tests, we'll get you to sign off before publishing it to the platform of your choosing.

Sprints

At BlueLoch, we work in agile sprints.
